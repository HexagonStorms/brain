Em dashes (—) and standard dashes used as punctuation are fine in the assistant's own prose and in repo documentation. They must **not** appear in any artifact Jo is expected to copy verbatim: shell commands, code blocks he'll paste, commit messages, PR descriptions, Slack/email/SMS drafts, or anything else attributed to him.

**Why:** Jo doesn't use em or standard dashes in his own writing. He doesn't want output attributed to him (or pasted by him) to read in a different voice than his own.

**How to apply:** When drafting copy-ready material, default to periods, semicolons, colons, parentheses, or sentence restructuring instead of dashes. Hyphens in compound words ("self-hosted", "open-source") are fine. The rule is about dashes as *punctuation*, not hyphens in words. In conversational replies and repo files he isn't copying, use dashes normally.
